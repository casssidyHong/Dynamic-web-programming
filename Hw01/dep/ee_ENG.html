Information

The Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering (ECE) at National Chiao Tung University (NCTU), now part of National Yang Ming Chiao Tung University (NYCU), is renowned for its strong academic tradition and cutting-edge research. Established in 1965, the department has played a pivotal role in cultivating top talents for Taiwan’s semiconductor and high-tech industries.

The ECE department offers a diverse curriculum that covers foundational topics in electronics, circuits, and systems, as well as advanced areas such as digital signal processing, communications, integrated circuit design, and artificial intelligence. Students gain a solid grounding in both theory and hands-on practice through laboratory courses and research opportunities, fostering a comprehensive understanding of electrical engineering principles.

With a focus on interdisciplinary learning, the department encourages collaboration between students and faculty members from various fields. Research is a core component of the ECE program, with faculty members leading projects in cutting-edge areas like semiconductor technology, wireless communications, and robotics. The department is equipped with modern laboratories and facilities that support both academic and industrial research, positioning students at the forefront of technological innovation.

Located in Hsinchu Science Park, known as Taiwan’s “Silicon Valley,” the ECE department is closely linked to the nation’s thriving technology sector. This unique location allows students to engage in collaborative projects with leading companies, participate in internships, and attend industry seminars, giving them a competitive edge in the job market.

Graduates from NCTU’s ECE department are highly regarded in both academia and industry, with many pursuing successful careers as engineers, researchers, and leaders in global technology firms. The department’s commitment to excellence and innovation makes it a premier choice for students aspiring to become pioneers in the field of electrical and computer engineering.
